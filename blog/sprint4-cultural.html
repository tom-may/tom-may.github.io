Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Simply put, Neuroplasticity is our brain's ability to adapt. By forming new pathways in our brain through learning, our brain changes, and adapts to new information. Our brain is malleable. So how can we utilise this to help aid our change? The first step to utilising this awesome power, is to understand what Neuroplasticity is. I think, therefore I am. I understand neuroplasticity, therefore I can use it to change my mindset and continue to grow. Awareness = endless possibility Neuroplasticity requires effort, so the old adage of “practice makes perfect” really holds true. With the knowledge that our brains can change and develop, no matter the stage of our lives, we can go forth in our lives with the knowledge that if we continue to push ourselves in our learning, we will continue to grow, and success is within our reach

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

While learning to code, I believe it will be fundamental to practice coding as much as possible, ideally every day. Neuroplasticity is not just about forming strong pathways in your brain, but it also describes that a lack of training can weaken these pathways. I have seen this directly when learning to touch type. If I practice for an hour every day, my competency improves at a steady rate, however, if I miss one or two days, I am set back from where I was before, and it takes time to catch up to the level I was at previously.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity

Practice, practice, practice - You need to keep on working and keep on challenging yourself. The more difficult the task, the greater the scope of learning potential. If you keep on doing easy challenges, sure it feels great to tick them off one by one, but you don’t really improve your learning.

Thinking about code and about challenges can also be beneficial. Just like visualizing a golf swing, or a line down the mountain on your snowboard can help when it comes to actually doing the activity, the same can be said for learning. Visualise and think about code after you have studied.

Just as visualisation and mental exercises help, reflection is also a great tool. So keep on reflecting after the fact.

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

Growth Mindset goes hand in hand with Neuroplasticity. Our brains are malleable and always changing. This is promoted even further by having a growth mindset. By understanding that we can continue to develop our brains by being open to change, we can work hard at achieving our goals, rather than just throwing in the towel when things get too challenging. If we believe that our brain development is fixed, we believe that we have already achieved a level of intelligence that is now fixed and unable to grow. With a growth mindset we believe that anything is possible if we work hard at it. Granted, everybody is different, being at different levels of growth, with different methods that work for development, but at the end of the day, when we know that we can grow, and we do not hold ourselves back, our possibilities become endless

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I found the case studies of classrooms in inner-city and indigenous regions of America to be quite fascinating. To see such stark results after only one year was very impressive. I have believed for a while now that anyone can achieve what they want when they work hard at it, but to now see studies proving so is absolutely awesome. It’s great to now have a name for it, and a better understanding so that I can focus on certain methods to improve my own learning.

How will you integrate a growth mindset into your learning journey?

By not being too critical of myself when my learning takes slower than I initially expected. By facing challenges with an understanding that the more difficult they are, the greater my learning potential. By not being idle in my work and reminding myself to keep trying new things and developing more. It is not the result that matters most, but the process by which the result was achieved.
