Identity, Values and Strenghts

An introduction to reflection

10 November 2020

When planning a hike through Nepal, my girlfriend and I were faced with a decision; Do we hire a guide, or do we self-direct?

With this came many more questions:

  • Is it ethical to have someone carry our bags for what is to us such a tiny expense?
  • Where are we going? Can we do it ourselves? Is it ethical and safe to do it ourselves?
  • Who benefits from our decision?

Ultimately, we decided to hike in a restricted area of the Himalayas that required a guide, but what about a porter to carry the bags as was offered by the tour guide? Well, we decided to get a porter too. However, while hiking we loaded our own bags with as much of our heavy gear as possible.

At the end of our 18 day hike through Nepal, I am very glad that we made the decision to hire both a guide and a porter. Along the way we met a hiker who was determined to “conquer the mountains” themselves, and was determined not to hire a guide or porter, despite warnings from locals about dangers on the trail. To each their own, but I believe that when travelling you gain so much through new experiences, and as a visitor to someone else's home, you are ultimately a tax on their resources in one way or another. Because of this, you have a responsibility to contribute what you can for the betterment of that society.

By engaging with a local hiking tour company and hiring a local guide and porter from the area in which we hiked, we contributed as much as we could directly to the area that we visited.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I now understand just how much of a privileged upbringing I have had, in a global context. Growing up in New Zealand it was so easy to take what we have here for granted. As a young nation and a cultural melting pot, we have a wonderful affinity for both acceptance of people from different backgrounds, and a connection to the outdoors, as well as a natural intrigue for what's beyond the horizon (both geographically and figuratively).

This is how I grew up and what shaped me as a young person. By going out into the big bad world, I gleaned from the varied experiences and ideas of others that I met along the way. I’m still growing, but I think I'm on the right track.

Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development

One strength that I think is very beneficial is that I am aware that I know nothing. I have a lot to learn and a lot of great people out there to learn from. As such, I am a sponge, ready to soak up all the knowledge I can.

A limitation is my ability to stay focused on a goal. Although adapting and pivoting to a new goal is often beneficial, when it comes to learning, I need a pretty rigid structure. This is where EDA comes in. I also often fail to reflect on my learnings and tend to move to the next task pretty quickly. As such, I need to focus on prioritizing and accounting for proper reflection time.

Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

Another of my strengths is passion. I tend to get very excited and swept up with an idea that grasps my attention. This will be very advantageous when working on personal projects. However, when I hit a creative block, this can become an obstacle. When I struggle, my passion wanes. I just need to remember to take a step back and look at the ’why’ I am doing this task. That will spark my motivation!
