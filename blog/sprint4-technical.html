Scooby… Dooby… Do.. How dare you.

And I would’ve gotten away with it, too, if it weren’t for your meddling code…

04 December 2020

Problem Solving

This week was an interesting one for me. I really enjoyed working through the kata’s, getting stuck, working towards a solution..., and BAM! You try a bit of code and it works! WooHoo!

But not only did I have to face the challenges of Javascript, but also the challenge of dislocating my shoulder. Ouchies. (It’s the fourth time it’s happened, so it happens pretty easily now.)

Funnily enough though, there are some similarities between dislocating your shoulder and getting blocked on a coding challenge. You have that initial “Oh cr*p” moment, and then you have to breathe, stop and think, ok, this has happened, what are my next steps. The solution to the problem is getting to the hospital, but there are a bunch of smaller steps that need to be taken first in order to achieve this goal. So you need a plan. I dislocated my shoulder at the pool while putting on my t-shirt after a swim (I told you it happens easily), so first of all I needed to get a lifeguard, then I needed to take of my t-shirt which was only half on, then get someone to go to my car to get my phone so that I could tell my girlfriend to meet me at the hospital. I then needed the lifeguard to drive my car to the hospital. Lots of steps to go through, all while thinking “OH F…….”

If there’s anything to be learnt from this experience, it’s that solving a problem requires calm, and quite often, help from others.

So when it came to finding Scooby, it required a lot of calm when I thought “oh cr*p”. I honestly had no idea where to begin. Hold up, what about console.logging? Let's take a look at what's coming through here. Right, an array. And what's in the array? An object? Ok let's Pseudocode and write down what to do from here. Now what about .find()? Lets try some googling?

Eventually, after a fair amount of research, guided by an understanding of exactly what I wanted to achieve, thanks to Pseudocode and rubber ducking, I wrote a piece of code that console.logged the ‘Scooby’ objects. From there it was a simple if statement to grab the door number. What a relief. Just like when the shoulder goes back in :D

Reflect on how confident you feel using the problem solving techniques and process:

Pseudocode

Love it. Every time I face a challenge I begin by writing some Pseudocode. I even put pen to paper from the most part because I’m old school like that.

Trying something

Always give something a try. It may not work at all, it may work in part, or it may be the solution. You won’t know until you try.

Rubber ducky method

I don’t have a rubber duck, but I do have a tiny plastic mouse holding a lightbulb. He’s great, we’re pals.

Reading error messages

Absolutely. Sometimes they can seem like gibberish, but for the most part, they let me know my syntax is wrong

Console.logging

Love love LOVE!

Googling

Ask Jeeves

How good. If you’ve got a problem, someone else has already solved it and posted about it online.

Asking your peers for help

We are all in this together, so we can all help each other learn. Everyone sees things differently, so we can all benefit from another's perspective

Asking coaches for help

I’m gonna rely on this a lot during bootcamp I think, but for now I’m trying to get cosy with most other techniques.

Improving your process with reflection

It can be hard to spend ample time reflecting at the moment, although I have been having some coding dreams lately. I’m really looking forward to having a good amount of time to reflect in the time before bootcamp.
